Assistant Professor · Reykjavik University

I am an Assistant Professor at Reykjavik University. My research lies at the intersection of human-computer interaction, natural language processing, and health informatics. I design and study conversational agents and virtual agents that support health behavior change, clinical assessment, and patient safety. I received my PhD from Northeastern University where I worked at the Relational Agents Group. I currently run the HUMI Lab, which is part of CADIA, the AI research center at Reykjavik University.
